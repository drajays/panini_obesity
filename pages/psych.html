Psychological & Behavioral Readiness Assessment

For patients starting chronic weight management with nutrition, activity and GLP‑1/GIP or GLP‑1 therapy. Chronic weight management relies heavily on behavioral adaptation. Identifying untreated psychological barriers early is critical. Screening tool only — not a diagnosis.

1) Mood & Anxiety Screening — PHQ‑9 and GAD‑7 baseline

Score each: 0=Not at all, 1=Several days, 2=More than half the days, 3=Nearly every day. Use to document baseline mental health stability and decide if added support/referral is needed.

PHQ‑9 Depression Screen — past 2 weeks

Item0123
1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
2. Feeling down, depressed or hopeless
3. Sleep trouble or sleeping too much
4. Feeling tired or having little energy
5. Poor appetite or overeating
6. Feeling bad about yourself / failure
7. Trouble concentrating
8. Moving/speaking slowly or being fidgety/restless
9. Thoughts you would be better off dead or self-harm
Total PHQ‑9
Safety: If PHQ‑9 item 9 >0, or any self-harm/suicide concern: urgent clinical assessment/referral.

GAD‑7 Anxiety Screen — past 2 weeks

Item0123
1. Feeling nervous, anxious or on edge
2. Not able to stop or control worrying
3. Worrying too much about different things
4. Trouble relaxing
5. Being so restless it is hard to sit still
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable
7. Feeling afraid as if something awful might happen
Total GAD‑7

Interpretation guide: PHQ‑9/GAD‑7 5=mild, 10=moderate, 15=moderately severe/severe range. Consider mental health support if ≥10, functional impairment, or clinician concern.

2) Binge Eating / Loss‑of‑Control Eating Screen

Episodes of eating an unusually large amount of food☐ Yes

Eating much faster than usual☐ Yes
Eating until uncomfortably full☐ Yes

Frequency: ☐ <weekly ☐ weekly+ ☐ most days; Duration: ☐ <3 mo ☐ ≥3 mo

Eating disorder red flags: vomiting, laxatives/diuretics, extreme restriction/fasting, compulsive exercise, fear of eating, rapid weight cycling, severe body-image distress. ☐ No ☐ Yes — consider specialist referral.

Ask the patient to define their “goal weight” and what they believe is required to get there. Use answers to correct unrealistic expectations early and align treatment with health, function and sustainability.
